About

I am a data scientist with a passion for storytelling. I believe that words and data are the two most powerful tools to change the world.

Most of my time is spent staring at a computer screen. During the day, I am usually programming, working to derive insight from large datasets. My skills include data analysis, visualization, and machine learning. I have developed a strong acumen for problem solving, and I enjoy an occasional challenge. I often work on end-to-end data science projects that usually begin from collecting data from third party sources and end with delivering business insight in the form of customer segments.

Outside of my professional work, I stay engaged with the data science community through research publications and continuous learning. I contribute to academic research on action rules and data mining, and I enjoy exploring new techniques in machine learning and statistical analysis. I also participate in online coding challenges to sharpen my problem-solving skills.

You can take a look at some of my projects and articles in the section below. I will link my work to their GitHub repositories, so feel free to download my code and play around with it. Most of my education has come from online platforms. I have downloaded e-books, audited courses on edX and Coursera, and spent countless hours on sites like HackerRank and LeetCode. I am grateful to online educators who have given me the opportunity to learn these things, and for democratizing education.

Research Publications

Support, Confidence and Utility of Action Rules Triggered by Meta-Actions

Research paper proposing improved measures for support and confidence of action rules, and introducing a new utility measure. The work employs meta-actions as nodes of higher-level knowledge linked with atomic action terms, showing changes triggered within classification attributes to improve action rule generation and pruning.

View Research Paper

Action Rules of Lowest Cost and Action Set Correlations

Research paper on action rule mining that introduces improved measures for support and confidence of action rules, along with a new utility measure. The work employs meta-actions as a pruning mechanism and addresses cost considerations in action rule discovery, providing a framework for identifying actionable patterns in decision systems.
