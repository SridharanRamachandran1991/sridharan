Research Publications

Support, Confidence and Utility of Action Rules Triggered by Meta-Actions

Imagine you have a magic recipe book that tells you how to change things for the better. This paper creates better ways to measure if those recipes actually work. It's like having three tests: How many people already succeeded using this recipe (support), how likely it is to work (confidence), and how many people could benefit from it (utility). The paper makes it easier to find the best recipes that really help people.

View Research Paper

Action Rules of Lowest Cost and Action Set Correlations

Think of this like finding the cheapest and easiest way to solve a problem. If you want to improve something, this paper helps you find the best plan that doesn't cost too much money or effort. It's like comparing different solutions and picking the one that gives you the most benefit for the least cost. The paper also looks at how different actions work together, like how eating healthy and exercising both help you get fit.
