Ecommerce Churn Analysis

Customer churn analysis using snapshot labeling (90-day window) to identify retention drivers across delivery performance, customer feedback, and geography.

Executive Summary

Snapshot churn label • 90-day window • Customer-level analysis

Overall churn rate: ~22.7% (customers with no repeat purchase in the next 90 days after the cutoff, based on 94,401 customers analyzed)
Delivery impact: Customers with frequent late deliveries churn at 75.2% vs 9.1% for customers with no late deliveries (+66.1 pp)
Reviews impact: Customers with low reviews (1–2) churn at 69.5% vs 11.2% for high reviews (4–5) (+58.2 pp)
Geography: Highest churn PA 77.3% vs lowest RN 10.3% (spread ~67.0 pp). Among large states, SP shows lowest churn at 20.9%.
Recommendation: Improve delivery reliability and proactively recover customers after poor experiences (late deliveries and low reviews).

Churn Definition

Snapshot churn label: Select a cutoff date, then label a customer as churned if they make zero purchases in the next 90 days.

Why this approach: It avoids inflated churn caused by the dataset ending (the “data stopped, so everyone churned” problem), and creates a clean retention label suitable for dashboards and modeling.

Project Overview

This project analyzes customer churn for the Olist Brazilian e-commerce dataset using a snapshot-based churn label. The goal is to identify actionable churn drivers and communicate them through stakeholder-friendly Tableau dashboards.

What I Built

  • Customer-level churn label using a 90-day snapshot window
  • Delivery reliability features (e.g., % of late deliveries per customer)
  • Customer experience features derived from review scores
  • Geographic churn segmentation to target retention programs by state
  • Tableau dashboards designed for fast exploration and decision-making

Key Questions

  • Where is churn highest geographically, and where should retention efforts focus?
  • Do late deliveries meaningfully increase churn risk?
  • Do poor customer reviews correlate with higher churn?

Method (High Level)

  • Prepared a customer-level dataset with order history, delivery performance, and reviews
  • Created a snapshot churn label: no purchases within 90 days after cutoff
  • Compared churn rates across segments (delivery buckets, review buckets, state)
  • Published results as Tableau dashboards with clear metrics and filters

Dashboard Analysis

Business Question

Which states show higher churn so retention and delivery improvements can be prioritized geographically?

Key Result

Churn varies dramatically across states, with a spread of ~67.0 percentage points. PA shows the highest churn (77.3%, 941 customers) while RN is lowest (10.3%, 470 customers). Among large states, SP (39,465 customers) shows the lowest churn at 20.9%, while MG (11,069 customers) shows high churn at 72.6%.

So What?

Use this view to focus retention investment where churn is highest, then drill into whether those states are driven by late deliveries, seller performance, or customer experience issues.

Business Recommendations

  • Investigate carriers/sellers contributing to late deliveries in high-churn states.
  • Localize customer support and delivery SLAs in problem regions.
  • Run targeted retention offers for high-churn states and measure lift.

Tip: Use the dashboard filters to isolate high-churn states, then compare delivery lateness and review score patterns.

Business Question

Does delivery reliability affect customer retention?

Key Result

Customers with frequent late deliveries churn at 75.2% (6,473 customers) vs 9.1% for customers with no late deliveries (87,903 customers) (+66.1 pp).

Interpretation

Late delivery is strongly associated with churn and is a practical retention lever because it can be influenced through carrier, seller, and process improvements.

Business Recommendations

  • Proactively message customers when delivery will be late to reduce frustration and support load.
  • Identify sellers/carriers driving late deliveries and introduce performance SLAs.
  • Create a "save offer" workflow after late deliveries (apology + coupon) and measure retention lift.

Tip: Toggle filters (late bucket / order count) to see how churn changes for repeat customers vs one-time buyers.

Business Question

Do negative customer reviews indicate higher churn risk?

Key Result

Customers with low reviews (1–2) churn at 69.5% (12,563 customers), compared with 11.2% for high reviews (4–5) (73,148 customers) (+58.2 pp). Mid-range reviews (3) show 52.9% churn (8,017 customers).

Interpretation

Review score is a useful proxy for customer experience and can be used for churn risk flagging and targeted retention actions.

Business Recommendations

  • Trigger support outreach or apology credits for low-review customers to reduce churn risk.
  • Monitor sellers/products with repeated low reviews for quality and fulfillment issues.
  • Use review score as a feature in a retention targeting model and track conversion over time.

Tip: Segment by review bucket and order frequency to identify the highest-risk customers for outreach.

Limitations & Next Steps

  • Correlation vs causation: These results show association; operational experiments would confirm causal impact.
  • Segment stability: Very small segments can be noisy; binned validation and minimum-sample filters help reduce this risk.

Next Steps

  • Build a churn risk model (logistic regression) and report AUC + precision/recall using a time-based split.
  • Create a “Retention Playbook” view: identify at-risk customers and recommended actions by segment.
  • Quantify potential lift: estimate churn reduction if late deliveries drop by X% and track impact via experiments.
